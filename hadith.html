Understanding Hadith

The second source of Islamic guidance after the Quran

H

What is Hadith?

Hadith are the recorded sayings, actions, and approvals of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). They provide practical guidance on how to implement the teachings of the Quran in daily life.

S

Hadith Sciences

Islamic scholars developed rigorous sciences to verify hadith authenticity, examining the chain of narrators (isnad) and the text (matn). Grades include Sahih (authentic), Hasan (good), and Da'if (weak).

6

The Six Books

The most authentic collections are: Sahih Al-Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, Sunan Abu Dawud, Jami At-Tirmidhi, Sunan An-Nasai, and Sunan Ibn Majah. These are known as the "Kutub al-Sittah."

Major Hadith Collections

Sahih Al-Bukhari

Compiler: Imam Muhammad al-Bukhari (810-870 CE)

Hadiths: 7,275 with repetitions (2,602 unique)

Considered the most authentic hadith collection. Imam Bukhari spent 16 years compiling it.

Sahih Muslim

Compiler: Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj (815-875 CE)

Hadiths: 7,500 with repetitions (3,033 unique)

Second most authentic collection. Known for its excellent organization.

Sunan Abu Dawud

Compiler: Imam Abu Dawud (817-889 CE)

Hadiths: 4,800

Focuses on legal rulings and jurisprudence matters.

Jami At-Tirmidhi

Compiler: Imam At-Tirmidhi (824-892 CE)

Hadiths: 3,956

Includes grading of each hadith and legal opinions of scholars.

Sunan An-Nasai

Compiler: Imam An-Nasai (829-915 CE)

Hadiths: 5,270

Known for strict criteria in selecting narrators.

Sunan Ibn Majah

Compiler: Imam Ibn Majah (824-887 CE)

Hadiths: 4,341

Completes the six major collections with unique hadiths.
