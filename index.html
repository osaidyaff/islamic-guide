What is Islam?

The basics of the world's fastest-growing religion

The Five Pillars

1. Shahada - Declaration of faith
2. Salah - Five daily prayers
3. Zakat - Charity to the needy
4. Sawm - Fasting in Ramadan
5. Hajj - Pilgrimage to Mecca

Core Beliefs

One God (Allah) - The Creator of all
Angels - Divine messengers
Holy Books - Including the Quran
Prophets - From Adam to Muhammad
Day of Judgment - Accountability for deeds

Peace & Mercy

Islam means "submission to God" and is derived from the word "Salam" (peace). Muslims greet each other with "Assalamu Alaikum" - Peace be upon you. The religion emphasizes mercy, justice, and compassion for all creation.

Remember Your Creator

أَلَا بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ تَطْمَئِنُّ الْقُلُوبُ

"Verily, in the remembrance of Allah do hearts find rest." The constant remembrance of Allah brings tranquility to troubled souls.

- Quran 13:28

Trust in Allah's Plan

فَإِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا

"For indeed, with hardship comes ease." Life's difficulties are temporary, and Allah's relief is always near for those who trust Him.

- Quran 94:5

Gratitude Transforms

لَئِن شَكَرْتُمْ لَأَزِيدَنَّكُمْ

"If you are grateful, I will surely increase you." Gratitude opens doors to more blessings and brings contentment to the heart.

- Quran 14:7
